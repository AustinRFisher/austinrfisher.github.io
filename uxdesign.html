UX DESIGN

EXPERIENCE

If my Bachelor’s degree in Global Business and Digital Arts (GBDA) taught me anything, it’s that UX Design is fundamental in everything we do.

User-centric design is key to creating good products, user testing ensures the project is on the right track, and the iterative process is key to launching with a polished, efficient product.

I’ve been exposed to UX design for 4 years now.

My experience with UX Design includes working through the iterative process and Agile workflows, creating mockups, conducting many rounds of user testing, drawing solutions, and enacting appropriate changes.

DESIGN INTERNSHIP

Over the summer of 2016, I worked as a Web and Print UX Designer at the Univesity of Waterloo’s Institutional Analysis and Planning (IAP) department. In this role, I conducted the following:

  • Website user testing (scenarios, interviews, out-loud)
  • Writing Strategic Plan copy (concise, clear)
  • Development, layout, and graphics of Strategic Plan (InDesign)
  • Graphic Design (Illustrator)

For the user testing, I analysed the IAP website to determine any potential flaws or concerns in user flow. I made a few hypotheses of cases I felt were problematic, and addressed those through scenarios in the user testing.

I kept some tasks open-ended for the user to voice their concerns through a vocal thinking process, in case there were problems I couldn’t spot.

Finally, I clarified the user’s position by interviewing them with precise questions to elaborate on comments made earlier in the test.

User Testing Summary Report

A breakdown of the testing performed, and the methodology behind the actionable conclusions.

This user test was conducted with 9 people, internally and externally. It supplied conclusive evidence that most areas of concern with the IAP website needed modification. My internship ended before I could implement the changes, unfortunately.

A/B TESTING

I conducted multiple iterations of A/B testing on one of my industry projects through GBDA called Emerge. The goal was to determine if it was better to have obvious or discrete sensors for an art installation. A/B testing determined that while labelled sensors were beneficial to learning, discrete sensors were better for discovery.

A second test was then conducted to see how users responded to influencing a piece of nature. A simple prototype was created to execute the test.

When testing the product shape of Quick Click, I enacted A/B testing to determine which shape of the project was easiest to grasp by target users. I then used a variation to determine what aesthetic form the product should take.

THE ITERATIVE PROCESS

Let’s not kid ourselves here. No idea is perfect the first time it’s thought up. That’s what makes the iterative process so crucial — being able to quickly analyze the scope of an idea, clarify the problems, conduct tests to solve these problems, using these solutions to develop the final product, rinse and repeat.

I’ve used the iterative process to execute many projects, including Quick Click and Emerge.
